# Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

These profiles describe the people we serve and what matters most to them. Use these when creating content, ads, or outreach to ensure messaging resonates.

---

## ICP 1: The Home Seller

**Who they are:**
A homeowner in LA or OC who has listed or is preparing to list their home for sale. They may be working with a real estate agent or selling independently.

**What they care about:**
- Closing the sale on time and without surprises
- Not spending more money than necessary
- Understanding what compliance means for their specific property
- Getting things done quickly so escrow can proceed

**Pain points:**
- Discovered compliance issues late in the transaction
- Confused about what California requires
- Worried about costs and delays
- Stressed about juggling the sale with daily life

**How we reach them:**
- Facebook ads and organic content targeted to homeowners
- Referrals from real estate agents
- Google Search (searching terms like "water heater strapping LA" or "smoke detector compliance escrow")

**Message that resonates:**
"We make compliance simple so your sale can close on time."

---

## ICP 2: The Real Estate Agent

**Who they are:**
A licensed real estate agent or broker in LA or OC who works with sellers. They handle multiple transactions at a time and depend on reliable vendors to protect their clients and their reputation.

**What they care about:**
- Vendors who are reliable and professional (they put their name on the line when they refer someone)
- Fast turnaround — delays cost everyone money
- Clear communication so they can update their clients
- Building a trusted vendor network

**Pain points:**
- Vendors who don't show up or communicate poorly
- Compliance surprises that derail transactions
- Clients who don't understand why compliance matters
- Having to explain compliance requirements themselves

**How we reach them:**
- LinkedIn (primary channel)
- Direct outreach to agents and brokerages
- Referral network building
- Educational content that helps them look knowledgeable

**Message that resonates:**
"We make you look good to your clients. Reliable, fast, and professional — every time."

## ICP 3: The Property Manager

**Who they are:**
A professional who manages residential rental properties across LA or OC. They oversee maintenance, compliance, and operations for multiple units or properties.

**What they care about:**
- Compliance across a portfolio, not just a single property
- Efficient scheduling (they can't babysit every vendor)
- Accurate reporting and documentation
- Cost efficiency at scale

**Pain points:**
- Managing compliance for many properties at once
- Keeping up with changing California requirements
- Vendors who waste their time with poor communication
- Gaps in documentation that create liability

**How we reach them:**
- LinkedIn (property management communities)
- Direct outreach to property management companies
- Referrals from real estate professionals

**Message that resonates:**
"We handle compliance across your portfolio so you don't have to think about it."

## ICP 4: The Informed Homeowner

**Who they are:**
A homeowner who is not actively selling but wants to ensure their property is compliant — for rental purposes, refinancing, or general safety and peace of mind.

**What they care about:**
- Understanding what's required
- Getting it done without a lot of hassle
- Safety for their family or tenants

**How we reach them:**
- Facebook content and ads
- Google Search
- Word of mouth

**Message that resonates:**
"Know your home is compliant before it becomes a problem."

---

## ICP 5: The Escrow Officer

**Who they are:**
A licensed escrow officer at an escrow company in LA or OC who manages the closing process for real estate transactions. They are the neutral third party responsible for ensuring all conditions of the sale are met before funds are disbursed and title transfers.

**What they care about:**
- Transactions closing on schedule
- All compliance conditions being satisfied with proper documentation
- Vendors who communicate clearly and deliver paperwork on time
- Minimizing delays that create liability or client complaints

**Pain points:**
- Compliance items that aren't resolved before the closing date
- Sellers who don't understand what's required or wait too long to act
- Vendors who are difficult to reach or slow to provide documentation
- Last-minute scrambles that push closing dates and upset all parties

**How we reach them:**
- Direct outreach to escrow companies in LA and OC
- Referrals from real estate agents they work with regularly
- LinkedIn (professional network with agents and title/escrow contacts)

**Referral opportunity:**
Escrow officers routinely encounter sellers who haven't addressed compliance. A trusted compliance vendor they can recommend on the spot is genuinely useful to them — it helps their transaction close cleanly. Building relationships with escrow officers is a high-value referral channel.

**Message that resonates:**
"We get it done and get you the paperwork. Your closing stays on track."
