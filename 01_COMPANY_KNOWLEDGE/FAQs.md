# Frequently Asked Questions

Use these Q&As for website copy, social media content, and AI-generated responses. Answers reflect California residential compliance requirements.

## General

**Q: What is a residential compliance inspection?**
A: A compliance inspection evaluates whether a home meets California's mandatory safety and conservation requirements for real estate transactions. These include smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors, water heater strapping, overflow pipes, low-flow fixtures, and seismic gas shutoff valves where required.

**Q: When do I need a compliance inspection?**
A: Most commonly before closing on a home sale. Sellers are required to ensure compliance prior to transfer of ownership. You may also need one for refinancing, converting a property to a rental, or simply for peace of mind.

**Q: Is compliance required by law in California?**
A: Yes. California law and local ordinances require specific safety and conservation features in residential properties. Failure to comply can delay or prevent the sale of a home.

## Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors

**Q: How many smoke detectors do I need?**
A: California requires smoke detectors in every bedroom, outside sleeping areas (hallways), and on every level of the home. Requirements for hardwired vs. battery-operated vary based on the age and type of property.

**Q: Do I need carbon monoxide detectors?**
A: Yes, if your home has an attached garage, gas appliances, or a fireplace. California has required CO detectors since 2011.

**Q: Can I use combination smoke/CO detectors?**
A: Yes, in many cases. We can advise on what meets local requirements for your property.

## Water Heater

**Q: What is water heater strapping and why is it required?**
A: Water heater strapping anchors the unit to the wall to prevent it from tipping or falling during an earthquake. California requires dual-strap systems. An unstrapped water heater can rupture a gas line in a quake — creating fire and explosion hazards.

**Q: What is an overflow pipe?**
A: The temperature-pressure relief (TPR) valve on your water heater requires a properly routed pipe that safely directs hot water away from people and the structure if the valve activates. Missing or improperly installed overflow pipes are a common compliance issue.

## Low-Flow Fixtures

**Q: What are low-flow plumbing requirements?**
A: California requires toilets to use no more than 1.6 gallons per flush, showerheads to use no more than 2.5 gallons per minute, and faucets to meet specific flow limits. Non-compliant fixtures must be replaced before a property can be sold.

---

## Seismic Gas Shutoff Valves

**Q: What is a seismic gas shutoff valve?**
A: A device installed on the gas line that automatically shuts off gas flow when it detects seismic activity above a threshold. This prevents gas leaks and fires after earthquakes.

**Q: Is a seismic shutoff valve required everywhere?**
A: Requirements vary by jurisdiction. Some cities in LA and OC require them; others do not. We evaluate whether one is required for your property.
