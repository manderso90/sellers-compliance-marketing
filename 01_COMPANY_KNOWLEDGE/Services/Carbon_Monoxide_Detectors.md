# Service: Carbon Monoxide Detector Evaluations and Installations

## What It Is

Evaluation of existing CO detectors for California compliance, and installation of new units where required.

## California Requirements

- Required since 2011 in all dwellings with an attached garage, gas appliances, or a fireplace
- Must be installed outside each sleeping area
- A single detector in a common area does not meet requirements for a multi-bedroom home

## What We Do

- Evaluate existing CO detectors for presence and placement
- Install new detectors where required

## Common Issues Found

- No CO detectors present (especially in older homes where it wasn't required at time of construction)
- Single detector installed for the whole house — not compliant for multi-bedroom layouts
- CO detector past its service life (typically 5–7 years)

## Combination Units

Combination smoke/CO detectors are acceptable in many applications. We advise on what meets local requirements for the specific property.

## Marketing Notes

Carbon monoxide is invisible and odorless. Many homeowners are unaware CO detectors are legally required — not just a good idea. Strong educational angle for social content.
