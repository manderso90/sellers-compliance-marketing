# Service: Low-Flow Plumbing Fixture Compliance

## What It Is

Evaluation and replacement of toilets, showerheads, and faucets that do not meet California's water conservation standards.

## California Requirements

| Fixture | Max Flow Rate |
|---|---|
| Toilets | 1.6 gallons per flush (gpf) |
| Showerheads | 2.5 gallons per minute (gpm) |
| Kitchen faucets | 2.2 gpm |
| Bathroom faucets | 2.2 gpm |

Fixtures manufactured before California's conservation standards were adopted often exceed these limits. Non-compliant fixtures must be replaced before a property can be sold.

## What We Do

- Evaluate all toilets, showerheads, and faucets for compliance
- Replace non-compliant fixtures with code-compliant units

## Common Issues Found

- Pre-1994 toilets (3.5 gpf or higher)
- Older showerheads exceeding 2.5 gpm
- Fixtures that were replaced but with non-compliant units from a hardware store

## Marketing Notes

Low-flow requirements are widely unknown among homeowners. Many assume their plumbing is "fine" because it works. The "your toilet from 1989 isn't legal to sell with" angle is a surprising and effective educational hook.
