# Service: Smoke Detector Evaluations and Installations

## What It Is

Evaluation of existing smoke detectors for California compliance, and installation of new or replacement units where required.

## California Requirements

- Required in every bedroom
- Required outside each sleeping area (hallways)
- Required on every level of the home, including basement
- As of 2014: single-family homes must use 10-year sealed-battery or hardwired units when replacing
- Smoke detectors expire — typically 10 years from manufacture date, printed on the unit

## What We Do

- Evaluate all existing detectors for presence, placement, and type
- Identify non-compliant or expired units
- Install compliant replacements where needed

## Common Issues Found

- Missing detectors (especially in older homes with additions)
- Battery-only units that don't meet current code
- Expired detectors (battery still works but unit is past service life)
- Incorrect placement (e.g., missing from a bedroom added during renovation)

## Marketing Notes

Smoke detectors are the compliance item most homeowners think they already have covered — and they're often wrong. The "expired detector" angle is a strong educational hook because it surprises people.
