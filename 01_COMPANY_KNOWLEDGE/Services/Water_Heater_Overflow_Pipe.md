# Service: Water Heater Overflow Pipe Installation

## What It Is

Installation or correction of the temperature-pressure relief (TPR) valve discharge pipe on residential water heaters.

## What It Does

The TPR valve is a safety device that releases pressure if the water heater overheats or over-pressurizes. The overflow pipe routes that discharge safely — away from people and away from areas that could cause damage.

## California Requirements

- TPR valve discharge pipe must be installed and properly routed
- Pipe must terminate at a safe location (not just dangling near the unit)
- Must be the correct diameter and material

## What We Do

- Evaluate existing overflow pipe installation
- Install or correct non-compliant discharge pipes

## Common Issues Found

- No discharge pipe present
- Pipe terminates in an unsafe location
- Pipe is undersized or made of inappropriate material
- Pipe was removed or disconnected during water heater replacement

## Marketing Notes

The overflow pipe is one of those items most homeowners have never heard of — which makes it a great educational content topic. Simple explanation: "your water heater has a pressure valve, and that valve needs somewhere safe to drain."
