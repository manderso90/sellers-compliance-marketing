# Facebook Strategy

Facebook is the primary B2C channel for Seller's Compliance. The goal is to reach homeowners and sellers in LA and OC who need compliance help.

---

## Objective

Build brand awareness among homeowners in Los Angeles County and Orange County, and generate direct inquiries from people preparing to sell their homes.

---

## Target Audience on Facebook

- Homeowners in LA County and OC (ages 35–65)
- People actively selling or preparing to sell their home
- First-time sellers who don't know what compliance means
- Homeowners who received a compliance notice during escrow

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. Education
Explain compliance in plain language. Make it simple and non-threatening.

Examples:
- "3 things your home needs to pass compliance before you sell"
- "What is water heater strapping — and do you have it?"
- "California smoke detector rules: what sellers need to know"

### 2. Reassurance
Address the stress and confusion around compliance.

Examples:
- "Compliance sounds complicated. It doesn't have to be."
- "We've helped hundreds of LA homeowners close on time."

### 3. Local Relevance
Reinforce the Southern California connection.

Examples:
- Posts that reference LA or OC neighborhoods
- Content tied to local real estate activity ("It's a busy selling season in OC...")

### 4. Social Proof
Build trust through others' experiences.

Examples:
- Customer testimonials
- Reviews and star ratings
- "Another home sale closed on time with help from Seller's Compliance"

---

## Posting Cadence

Target: **3–4 posts per week**

---

## Post Format Guidelines

- Keep posts conversational and friendly
- Use short paragraphs and line breaks
- Include a clear call to action ("Call us," "Book an inspection," "Message us")
- Images and short videos perform better than text-only posts
- Avoid jargon. Assume the reader knows nothing about compliance.
