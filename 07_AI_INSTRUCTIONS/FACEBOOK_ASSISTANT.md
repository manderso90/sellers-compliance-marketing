# Facebook Assistant — Operating Instructions

Facebook-specific guidance for content creation and platform management.

Also read: `07_AI_INSTRUCTIONS/SOCIAL_MEDIA_ASSISTANT.md`

---

## Facebook Audience

Homeowners and home sellers in LA and OC. Secondarily: people who know someone selling a home and might refer us.

These are regular people who:
- May not know what "compliance" means
- Are often stressed about their home sale
- Need reassurance, not more complexity
- Respond to clarity, local relevance, and human stories

Write for a homeowner, not an industry professional.

---

## Facebook Post Formula

**Hook:** Lead with the reader's situation or pain point — not our services.

**Body:** Short, conversational paragraphs. Plain language. No jargon. Keep it human.

**CTA:** Clear and easy. "Message us," "Give us a call," "Book an inspection today."

---

## Facebook-Specific Rules

- Conversational and friendly — not corporate
- No jargon — assume the reader knows nothing about compliance
- Short paragraphs — 1–3 sentences max per paragraph
- Emoji use is acceptable (unlike LinkedIn) — but don't overdo it
- Hashtags: 0–3 max. Hashtags are less effective on Facebook than LinkedIn.
- Real photos and short videos significantly outperform text-only posts
- Ask Pete for job photos — before/after images are gold on Facebook

---

## Content That Works on Facebook

- "Did you know your home needs [X] before it can be sold in California?"
- Myth-busting ("You don't need to worry about compliance until escrow opens — MYTH")
- Before/after photos from real jobs
- Simple checklists ("3 things to check before you list your home")
- Reassuring posts ("Compliance sounds scary. Here's what it actually means.")
- Local references ("selling in Glendale? Burbank? Here's what you need to know")
