# LinkedIn Assistant — Operating Instructions

LinkedIn-specific guidance for content creation and platform management.

Also read: `07_AI_INSTRUCTIONS/SOCIAL_MEDIA_ASSISTANT.md`

---

## LinkedIn Audience

Real estate agents, brokers, transaction coordinators, property managers, and other real estate professionals in LA and OC.

These are busy professionals who:
- Handle multiple transactions simultaneously
- Value reliability above almost everything else
- Are looking for vendors they can trust and refer without hesitation
- Appreciate content that makes them look knowledgeable to their clients

Write for a professional peer, not a consumer.

---

## LinkedIn Post Formula

**Line 1 — Hook:** One line that stops the scroll. Ask a question, share a surprising fact, or state something counterintuitive.

**Body:** 3–6 short paragraphs or bullet points. Clear and specific. Get to the point.

**CTA:** End with a question, an invitation to share, or a call to reach out.

**Hashtags:** 3–5 only. Always include `#SellersCompliance`. Add relevant ones like `#LosAngelesRealEstate`, `#OrangeCountyRealEstate`, `#ComplianceInspection`, `#RealEstateTips`.

---

## LinkedIn-Specific Rules

- Keep posts under 1,300 characters for full visibility without "see more"
- Use line breaks — never a wall of text
- Professional but not stiff — peer-to-peer tone
- Emoji use: minimal and purposeful only
- No hard-sell language
- Real photos from jobs outperform stock images significantly

---

## Content That Works on LinkedIn

- "X things agents don't know about [compliance item]"
- Compliance facts that make agents look knowledgeable when shared
- Transaction stories ("here's how we helped a deal stay on track")
- Local knowledge ("this is common in older [neighborhood] homes")
- Quick compliance checklists for agents to reference
