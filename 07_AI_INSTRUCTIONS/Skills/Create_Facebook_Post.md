# Skill: Create Facebook Post

**Triggered by:** `/facebook-post` command

---

## Purpose

Draft a Facebook post for Seller's Compliance targeting homeowners and sellers in LA and OC.

---

## Inputs

Provide one of the following:

- **Topic:** A compliance topic or service to highlight
- **Pillar:** Education / Reassurance / Local Relevance / Social Proof
- **Audience:** If this post targets a specific persona (home seller, first-time seller, homeowner)

---

## Process

1. **Review the audience.** Facebook posts target homeowners and sellers — not industry professionals. Write for someone who doesn't know what compliance means and may be stressed about their transaction.

2. **Check existing content.** Review `04_CONTENT_LIBRARY/` for existing educational content or tips to adapt.

3. **Draft the post.**

   Structure:
   - **Hook:** Lead with the reader's situation or pain point, not our services.
   - **Body:** Short, conversational, plain language. No jargon.
   - **CTA:** Clear next step ("Message us," "Book an inspection," "Call today").

4. **Apply brand voice check:**
   - Is it friendly and approachable?
   - Is it free of industry jargon?
   - Would a homeowner who doesn't know what compliance is understand this?
   - Is there a clear reason to contact us?

5. **Output the draft** ready for review. Save drafts to `03_SOCIAL_MEDIA/Facebook/Drafts/`.

---

## Example Output Format

```
[Hook — speak to their situation]

[Body — simple, helpful, reassuring]

[CTA — clear and easy]
```

---

## Notes

- Facebook allows longer posts but shorter performs better for organic reach.
- Avoid hashtag overuse on Facebook (1–3 max, or none).
- Emoji use is acceptable on Facebook (unlike LinkedIn where it should be used sparingly).
- Do not invent compliance rules.
