# Skill: Turn Photos Into Content

**Triggered by:** `/photo-analysis [description of photos]`

---

## Purpose

Take job photos from Pete and turn them into social media post concepts and captions for LinkedIn and/or Facebook.

---

## Inputs

- **Photos** (shared directly, or described: "before photo of unstrapped water heater, after photo with straps installed")
- **Job details** (what service was performed, any relevant context)
- **Target platform** (LinkedIn, Facebook, or both)

---

## Process

1. **Identify the story.** What does this photo show? What problem was solved? What is the before/after narrative?

2. **Match to content pillars.**
   - Before/after photos → Education + Social Proof
   - Installation photos → Behind the Scenes
   - Completed job → Social Proof

3. **Draft for the right platform(s).**
   - **LinkedIn:** Lead with the professional insight ("Here's what a properly strapped water heater looks like — and why it matters for your listings")
   - **Facebook:** Lead with the homeowner story ("This is what we fixed for a seller in [area] before their home went into escrow")

4. **Apply platform rules.**
   - See `07_AI_INSTRUCTIONS/LINKEDIN_ASSISTANT.md`
   - See `07_AI_INSTRUCTIONS/FACEBOOK_ASSISTANT.md`

5. **Output drafts** ready for review. Save to appropriate `Drafts/` folder.

---

## Output

- 1–2 draft posts per platform requested
- Suggested caption for each
- Note if the photo would work better for one platform vs. the other

---

## Tips

- Before/after photos are among the highest-performing content types on both platforms
- Even a single quality job photo can generate 3–5 content pieces across platforms and pillars
- Pete's photos from the field are irreplaceable — stock images don't build the same trust
